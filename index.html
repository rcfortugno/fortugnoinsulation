Frequently Asked Questions

What is "R-Value"?

R-Value is a measure of thermal resistance used in the building and construction industry. It indicates how well a layer of insulation can resist heat flow. The higher the R-Value, the better the insulation performance.

Factors affecting R-Value include:

  • Type of insulation material
  • Thickness of insulation
  • Density of insulation
  • Installation quality

Our experts will assess your home's current R-Value and recommend improvements to maximize energy efficiency. We ensure that your insulation meets or exceeds local building codes and energy standards.

How much can I save on energy bills with proper insulation?

Savings can vary widely depending on your current insulation levels and energy usage. On average, our customers see savings ranging from 15% to 40% on their energy bills after our insulation services.

How long does the insulation process take?

The duration varies depending on the size of your home and the extent of work needed. Most residential projects are completed within 1-2 days.

How often should insulation be replaced?

The lifespan of insulation can vary, but generally, it should be inspected every 15-20 years. Factors like moisture, pests, or settling can necessitate earlier replacement.
